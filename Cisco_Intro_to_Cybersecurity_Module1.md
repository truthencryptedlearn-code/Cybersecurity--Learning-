# Cisco Introduction to Cybersecurity - Module 1: Key Points

## 1.1.1 What Is Cybersecurity?
- Cybersecurity = protecting computers, networks, and data from attacks.
- Goal: Keep information **safe and private**.
- Prevent hacking, data loss, malware, viruses.

## 1.1.2 Protecting Your Personal Data
- Personal data = name, email, password, photos, messages.
- Protect with **strong passwords** and **two-factor authentication (2FA)**.
- Avoid sharing sensitive info publicly.

## 1.1.3 Your Online Identity
- Online identity = how you appear online.
- Hackers can **steal or impersonate** your identity.
- Use privacy settings and be careful what you share.

## 1.1.4 Your Data
- Data = everything you create, share, or store online.
- Examples: documents, photos, messages, browsing history.
- Protect sensitive data with **secure passwords and backups**.

## 1.1.5 Where Is Your Data?
- Stored on: devices, cloud storage, social media, apps.
- Know **where your data is** and who can access it.
- Use **secure networks** to keep it safe.

## 1.1.6 What's More...
- Other risks: malicious apps, suspicious emails, downloads.
- Always check **app permissions**.
- Avoid clicking unknown links or attachments.

## 1.1.7 Smart Devices
- Smart devices = phones, smart TVs, smart watches, IoT devices.
- They **collect and store data** about you.
- Protect devices: **passwords, updates, secure networks**.

## 1.1.8 What Do Hackers Want?
- Hackers may want: money, personal info, system access.
- Common methods: phishing, malware, social engineering.
- Awareness = first step to prevention.

## 1.1.9 Identity Theft
- Identity theft = someone **pretends to be you** to steal money or info.
- Protect by monitoring accounts and reporting suspicious activity.
- Never share passwords or sensitive data.

## 1.1.10 Who Else Wants My Data?
- Companies, advertisers, governments may collect your data.
- Uses: marketing, analytics, service improvements.
- Read privacy policies and **control permissions**.
